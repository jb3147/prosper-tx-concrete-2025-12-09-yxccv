Frequently Asked Questions

Have questions about pricing, timelines, or what to expect? These answers cover the most common topics we discuss with Prosper homeowners and builders.

Pricing depends on several factors, including square footage, thickness, site access, soil conditions, and whether you choose standard, stained, or stamped finishes. Most residential driveway or patio projects we complete in Prosper fall within a clear price range once we review your site and design preferences. We'll visit your property, take measurements, discuss options, and then provide a detailed written estimate that breaks down labor, materials, and any optional upgrades—so you can make an informed decision without guesswork.

Most single‑family driveway or patio projects take 2–4 days of on‑site work, plus curing time before full use. Larger foundations, pool decks, or commercial slabs can take longer, especially if multiple pours or inspections are involved. During your estimate, we'll outline a realistic schedule based on scope, permitting, and weather. We prioritize accurate timelines, and our 98% on‑time completion rate reflects that commitment.

Long‑lasting concrete starts long before the truck arrives. We evaluate your soil, plan for drainage, and specify the right mix design, slump, and reinforcement for your application. Our crews compact the base, set forms with correct slope, install rebar or wire mesh, and place control joints where they'll minimize cracking. We also monitor weather conditions on pour day and follow proper curing and sealing practices. These professional steps dramatically reduce the risk of premature cracking, heaving, or surface failure compared to a basic DIY or low‑bid installation.

For most projects within Prosper city limits, we can assist with or fully manage the permitting and inspection process, depending on the scope of work. We're familiar with local requirements for driveways, approaches, and structural slabs, and we design our work to meet or exceed those standards. During our initial consultation, we'll clarify what permits, if any, are needed and outline whether they're included in our proposal or handled directly by you or your builder.

Quality installation is the first step; proper maintenance is the second. We'll provide care guidelines specific to your project, but generally we recommend avoiding heavy loads during the initial curing period, cleaning spills promptly, and resealing decorative finishes every few years. Avoid harsh de‑icing chemicals and metal shovels that can damage the surface. With basic care, most of our residential concrete installations provide 20–30+ years of dependable service.
